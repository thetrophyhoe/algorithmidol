OFFICIAL RULES — ALGORITHM IDOL

“The Algorithm Saves You. The Humans Vote You Out.”
Presented by Glitched Records | Hosted by the Trophy Hoe

Season Overview

  • 10-week TikTok competition (Week 1 Purge + 8 competition weeks + live Finale).
  • 100% AI-generated music only (Suno, Udio, stems + custom prompts, etc.), except for the one secret human contestant.
  • One secret human contestant (traditional DAW, zero AI) hidden among the 50 entrants — the “Find the Glitch” twist.
  • All songs must be in English (80%+ lyrics) for fair voting and judging.
  • Open to creators 18+.
  • Identity verification required prior to prize delivery.

Week 1: The Great Purge (50 → 25)

  • Open submissions to 50 contestants.
  • Submissions open from announcement until Wednesday the week prior to show start.
  • Combined score elimination of Algorithm + Judges reduces the field from 50 to 25.
  • We encourage all 50 artists to make full use of their network for likes, shares, comments, duets, and live views from day one.

Core Format (Weeks 2–10)

  1. Safety Round (Judges + Algorithm)
    5 human judges score each track (1–10). Scores averaged into Panel Score.
    Algorithm Score contributes 50% to the combined safety score.
    Combined score = Top X safe (“The Algorithm has spoken — you’re safe!”).
  2. Elimination Round (Pure Human Vote)
    Bottom group goes to public vote only.
    Fans must vote using the hashtag #Save[contestantname]week[number].
    Lowest vote-getter is eliminated.

NEXUS — The AI Judge

After safety is announced, NEXUS (the living algorithm entity) awakens and injects ONE GLITCH POWER that carries into the next challenge.

The Secret Human Twist — “Find the Glitch”

One of the 50 original contestants is 100% human (traditional DAW, zero AI tools).
The human is treated exactly like every other contestant.
Judges and audience do not know who it is until the Finale reveal.
Launch / Week 1: “50 Artists. 1 is Human. Find the Glitch.”
Finale (Top 3): Winner announced first. Then — if the human made Top 3 — they step forward and reveal: “I never touched AI once.”

This twist settles the “AI has no soul” debate in public, on camera, forever.
